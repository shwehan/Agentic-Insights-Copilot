# Segmentation Definitions

Segmentation is the process of dividing a broader population into meaningful subgroups based on shared characteristics, behaviors, needs, or value.

Common segmentation goals:
- identify target populations
- understand behavioral differences
- prioritize strategy opportunities
- tailor engagement or messaging
- support profitability, retention, or experience analysis

# Sample Variable Groups

## Demographic / household context
- age band
- life stage
- household size
- geography
- urban vs suburban vs rural

## Product / account behavior
- product ownership
- number of accounts
- category usage patterns
- transaction activity
- balance behavior

## Engagement / channel usage
- mobile usage
- online usage
- email engagement
- service interaction frequency
- digital adoption

## Financial / value signals
- profitability
- spend volume
- product depth
- average balance
- lifetime value proxy

## Survey / attitudinal signals
- satisfaction score
- ease-of-use score
- service quality perception
- loyalty / advocacy score
- feature usefulness rating

# Common Output Sections

A good segmentation brief should include:
- business objective
- target population
- recommended segmentation logic
- candidate variables
- suggested method
- expected analysis outputs
- assumptions and risks

# Simple Business Rules

Use rule-based segmentation when:
- business definitions are clear
- explainability matters
- stakeholders need interpretable output
- population logic is already partially known

Use clustering when:
- the business question is exploratory
- behavioral groupings are unclear
- multiple dimensions need to be considered together
- discovering natural patterns is useful

Use a hybrid approach when:
- some business rules are fixed
- exploratory grouping is still needed inside a defined population

# Example Use Cases

## Digital engagement segmentation
Goal:
Identify distinct groups based on digital behavior and channel preferences.

Potential signals:
- mobile activity
- online session frequency
- service channel usage
- email engagement
- digital feature adoption

## Customer value and activity segmentation
Goal:
Separate customers by behavioral engagement and economic value.

Potential signals:
- transaction frequency
- spend volume
- product holdings
- average balance
- profitability measures

## Rewards strategy segmentation
Goal:
Identify meaningful customer groupings for rewards design, targeting, or optimization.

Potential signals:
- category spend mix
- product ownership patterns
- reward usage behavior
- profitability
- engagement intensity

## Customer experience survey segmentation
Goal:
Understand how different customer groups respond to service and product experience surveys.

Potential signals:
- satisfaction score
- ease-of-use score
- response patterns
- service channel usage
- product depth

## Retention and lifecycle segmentation
Goal:
Identify customer groups that differ in retention risk, growth potential, or lifecycle stage.

Potential signals:
- tenure
- activity trends
- product adoption patterns
- service contact history
- value trajectory
